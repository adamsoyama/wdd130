Nigeria

Port Harcourt, located in Nigeria's Niger Delta, is known as the "Garden City" for its parks and green spaces. It's the capital of Rivers State and a hub for the oil and gas industry. The city features a diverse cultural scene, vibrant markets, and a lively nightlife. It hosts the University of Port Harcourt, international companies, and a busy port vital to the economy. The picturesque waterfront and Port Harcourt Tourist Beach offer serene escapes.

Founded in 1912 by the British, Port Harcourt has grown into a cultural melting pot, seen in its cuisine, festivals, and daily life. The city is known for events like the Port Harcourt Carnival, with colorful parades and traditional music. Well-connected by road, sea, and air, the Port Harcourt International Airport serves as a major gateway, balancing historical charm with modern amenities, making it a top destination for business and leisure.
